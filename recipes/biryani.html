Biryani

Description

Biryani is a mixed rice dish originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is made with Indian spices, rice, and usually some type of meat (chicken, beef, goat, lamb, prawn, fish) or in some cases, eggs or vegetables such as potatoes and carrots.

Biryani is one of the most popular dishes in South Asia and among the diaspora from the region. The main ingredients are rice, meat, spices, and sometimes, vegetables. The dish is cooked in a special way that makes it flavorful and aromatic.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 pound chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
